    Literal Translation (Word‑for‑Word)
    “Ik‑Onkar, Sat‑Naam, Karta, Purakh, Nirbhao, Nirvair, Akāl, Mūrat, Ajūni, Sabh, Guru‑Prasad.”

    Spiritual Interpretation

  • Ik‑Onkar – “One Supreme Reality.”

    • The Guru reminds us that there is a single, indivisible divine source. All creation is a manifestation of this One.

  • Sat‑Naam – “Truth‑Name.”

    • God’s essence is truth. By remembering His true name we align our thoughts and actions with divine reality.

  • Karta‑Purakh – “The Creator, the Person.”

    • God is not just a force; He is a personal, conscious Being who created everything.

  • Nirbhao‑Nirvair – “Fearless, without hatred.”

    • The divine is free from all negative emotions. When we follow His example, we learn to live without fear or enmity.

  • Akāl – “Eternal.”

    • God exists beyond time. His presence is constant, unchanging, and beyond birth or death.

  • Mūrat‑Ajūni – “Image, self‑existent.”

    • God’s form is neither created nor destroyed; He is self‑sustaining and the source of all forms.

  • Sabh – “All‑Being.”

    • God is the essence of everything. All existence is a reflection of His presence.

  • Guru‑Prasad – “By the Guru’s grace.”

    • Though God is infinite, the Guru is the instrument through which we receive His blessings. The Guru’s grace makes it possible to remember and experience the divine.

    Daily Life Applications

    LessonPractical Action for a New Day
    Remember the OneBegin the day with a simple affirmation: “Ik‑Onkar.” This centers your mind on unity and steadiness.
    Speak TruthSpeak with honesty and integrity. Let “Sat‑Naam” guide your words.
    Act with CompassionTreat everyone as an expression of the same divine essence. This reduces fear and hatred.
    Live in the PresentRecognize that “Akāl” is ever‑present; focus on the now rather than worrying about past or future.

    Practice Mindful SilenceSpend a few minutes in quiet reflection; this mirrors the “Mūrat‑Ajūni” quality of being self‑contained and still.
