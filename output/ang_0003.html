    2. Literal Translation

    Stanza 9 (numbers 9‑10)

    Listening‑pain and sin are erased.

    Listening‑truth, contentment and spiritual wisdom.

    Listening‑take your cleansing bath at the sixty‑eight places of pilgrimage.

    Listening‑reading and reciting, honor is obtained.

    Listening‑intuitively grasp the essence of meditation.

    O Nanak, the devotees are forever in bliss.

    Stanza 10 (numbers 10‑11)

    Listening‑pain and sin are erased.

    Listening‑dive deep into the ocean of virtue.

    Listening‑the Shaykhs, religious scholars, spiritual teachers and emperors.

    Listening‑even the blind find the Path.

    Listening‑the Unreachable comes within your grasp.

    O Nanak, the devotees are forever in bliss.

    Stanza 11 (numbers 11‑12)

    The state of the faithful cannot be described.

    One who tries to describe this shall regret the attempt.

    No paper, no pen, no scribe can record the state of the faithful.

    Such is the Name of the Immaculate Lord.

    Only one who has faith will attain this state.

    (Because the passage is a poetic recitation, the literal English translation preserves the “listening‑” prefix that appears before each line, underscoring the Guru’s insistence that listening to the Naam is the key to every truth that follows.)

    3. Spiritual Message

    ThemeInsight
    The Power of ListeningBy suniaai (listening to the Naam), pain and sin are erased; truth, contentment, and knowledge flow naturally.
    Ineffability of the FaithfulThe “faithful” (man ke) are beyond words; no earthly record can capture their state. Their existence is a living testament to the One Name.
    The “Chosen” (Sahakari)Those who sing the Naam, irrespective of status (Sikh, teacher, emperor, blind), are all “chosen.” Their union with the divine is inevitable.
    Cosmic OrderThe entire universe is woven from the One Name; the “record” (lekhaa) of creation is the divine word itself.
    Practice of DevotionListening, humility, faith, and mindful action are the four pillars that sustain the devotee’s eternal bliss.
